How to say in Japanese "I can / can't / could / couldn't"

In this lesson, you will learn to say "I can go to the movies today," "how can we help?" etc. That is, phrases with the verb "can". To do this, we'll have to cover some grammar stuff.

First of all, let us understand what a verb is in Japanese. We have already gotten used to the fact that in the sentence, a verb is the word that ends with a bunch of ます. So, this form is called the "personal neutral and polite form of the verb", or in other words, "the verb in a certain person". It is the same as in Russian the verb "do" or "do". If we need to say "buy" in Japanese instead of "I buy" it will be an infinitive and no longer 買います, but 買う . It is in this form that you will find this verb in a common Japanese-Russian dictionary. It is called a verb in a dictionary form.

In infinitive, or impersonal form (i.e. "do"), the verb always ends with a letter U of the Japanese Kana alphabet. These are the combinations with U that the Japanese verb can end in:


うu 　⇒　 もmoらraうu (to receive) 　⇒　 もmoらraいiまmaすsu (receive/receives)
くku 　⇒　 聞kiくku (to listen) 　⇒　 聞kiきkiまmaすsu (listen/listens)
ぐgu 　⇒　 泳oyoぐgu (to swim) 　⇒　 泳oyoぎgiまmaすsu (swim/swims)
つtsu 　⇒　 持moつtsu (to hold) 　⇒　 持moちchiまmaすsu (hold/holds)
ぬnu 　⇒　 死shiぬnu (to die) 　⇒　 死shiにniまmaすsu (die/dies)
すsu 　⇒　 話hanaすsu (speak) 　⇒　 話hanaしshiまmaすsu (speak/speaks)
むmu 　⇒　 読yoむmu (to read) 　⇒　 読yoみmiまmaすsu (read/reads)
ぶbu 　⇒　 呼yoぶbu (to call) 　⇒　 呼yoびbiまmaすsu (call/calls)
るru 　⇒　 見miるru (to look) 　⇒　 見miまmaすsu (look/looks)

The form of verbs with "can" is formed differently for different groups of verbs (and there are three in total). Let's have a look at how it happens.


First group

These are the verbs that end in vocabulary at う,く,す,つ,ぬ,ぶ,む,ぐ. Accordingly, the words before ます are based on the letter from row i (い、き、し、み、り、ち、に).

買kaいiまmaすsu　 (買kaうu) – to buy

書kaきkiまmaすsu　 (書kaくku) – to write

行iきkiまmaすsu　 (行iくku) – to go


The verbs of the first group have the form with "can" as follows: we change the letter from row i preceding ます to the corresponding letter from row e (え、けk、せ、て、ね、め、れ).

Examples:

　　 　　 　 　 　
会aいiまmaすsu
(I meet)		 　⇒　会aえeまmaすsu
(I can meet)		 　⇒　 会aえeまmaせseんn
(I can't meet)
書kaきkiまmaすsu
(I write)		 　⇒　 書kaけkeまmaすsu
(I can write)		 　⇒　 書kaけkeまmaせseんn
(I can't write)
貸kaしshiまmaすsu
(I lend)		 　⇒　貸kaせseまmaすsu
(I can lend)　 		　⇒　貸kaせseまmaせseんn
(I can't lend)
待maちchiまmaすsu　
(I wait)		 　⇒　待maてteまmaすsu
(I can wait)　 		　⇒　待maてteまmaせseんn
(I can't wait)
飛toびbiまmaすsu
(I fly)		 　⇒　 飛toべbeまmaすsu
(I can fly)		 　⇒　 飛toべbeまmaせseんn
(I can't fly)

今日kyou、とtoもmoだdaちchiにni　 会aえeまmaせseんn。– Today I will not be able to meet my friends.


Second group

These are the verbs that always end in the dictionary form at る, which must be preceded by an E or I sound, i.e. syllables え、け、せ、ち、に、び, etc.

食taべbeまmaすsu (I eat)　( 食taべbeるru) – (to eat)

教oshiえeまmaすsu (I explain)　( 教oshiえeるru) – (to explain)


まmaすsu　⇒　らraれreまmaすsu


Examples:

食taべbeまmaすsu (I eat)　⇒　食taべbeらraれreまmaすsu (I can eat)　⇒　食taべbeらraれreまmaせseんn (I can't eat)

寝neまmaすsu (I sleep)　⇒　寝ne らraれreまmaすsu (I can sleep)　⇒　寝ne らraれreまmaせseんn (I can't sleep)


Thrid group

This includes the exception verbs 来る（来ます）- come and する（します）- do, and all the compound verbs formed with them.


NOUN + しshiまmaすsu


結kek婚kon (marriage)　+　しshiまmaすsu　=　結kek婚kon しshiまmaすsu (to marry)

勉ben強kyou (study)　+　しshiまmaすsu　=　勉ben強kyouしshiまmaすsu (to syudy)

ディdiスsuカkaウuンnトto (discount)　+　しshiまmaすsu　=　ディdiスsuカkaウuンnトto しshiまmaすsu (to make a discount)

For example, in these examples, the words 結婚 (marriage) and 勉強 (study) are kango and a ディスカウント (discount) is gairaigo.

Or with the verb 来ます:

持moっtてte (to hold)　+　来kiまmaすsu　=　 持moっtてte来kiまmaすsu (to carry),

連tsuれreてte (to appertain)　+　来ki まmaすsu　=　 連tsuれreてte来kiまmaすsu (to bring someone/something).


勉ben強kyouしshiまmaすsu (I study)　⇒　勉ben強kyouでdeきkiまmaすsu (I can study)

持moっtてte来kiまmaすsu (I bring)　⇒　持moっtてte来koらraれreまmaすsu (I can bring)


こkoのno本hon　をo　読yoみmiまmaすsu。– I read this book.

こkoのno本hon　がga　読yoめmeまmaすsu。– I can read this book.


こkoのno本hon　はwa　読yoめmeまmaせseんn。– I can't read this book.


行iけkeまmaすsu (I go)　⇒　行i けkeまmaせseんn (I can't go)

食taべbeらraれreまmaすsu(I eat)　⇒　食ta べbeらraれreまmaせseんn (I can't eat)

来koらraれreまmaすsu (I come)　⇒　来ko らraれreまmaせseんn (I can't come)


Questions

To do this, we simply add か at the end (if the answer to the question is yes or no) or substitute a question word (if we expect to hear new information in response).

ジjョoーuジji　さsaんn、 漢kan字ji　がga　 書kaけkeまmaすsu　かka。– George, can you write hieroglyphs?
