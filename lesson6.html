How to say "I want", "I wanted" in Japanese

You can express your wish with the suffix たい (i.e. we want to do something: want + verb) or with the adjective ほしい (we just want something: want + noun). The grammar is very simple - let's consider these two constructions separately.

If we want to do something (I want to + verb), for example if we want to dance, in this case a construction with the suffix たい is used. It is joined by the following formula:


verb (without copula まmaすsu) + たtaいi


Examples:

飲noみmiまmaすsu (I drink / I am drinking) - 飲noみmiたtaいi (I want to drink)

見miまmaすsu (I watch / I see) - 見miたtaいi (I want to see)

しshiまmaすsu (I do / I'm doing) - しshiたtaいi (I want to do)

来kiまmaすsu (I come / I'm coming) - 来kiたtaいi (I want to come)


After たい, a polite form is followed by a verb-link (copula) です.

来rai年nen、日ni本honへe　(休yasuみmiにni)　行iきkiたtaいi　でdeすsu。– Next year I want to go to Japan (on vacation).

あaのno　高takaいiビbiルruにni　住suみmiたtaいi　でdeすsu。– I want to live in that tall building.

テteレreビbiでde　働hataraきkiたtaいi　でdeすsu。– I want to work on television.

If in the simple sentence "I'm watching (who? what?) TV" we use a noun with the particle を before the verb:

テteレreビbi　でde　 コkoンnサsaーaトto　をo　 見miまmaすsu。 – I'm watching a concert on TV.

then, when we use the form at たtい, the を particle may change to が, but more often を is used.

テteレreビbi　でde　コkoンnサsaーaトto　 がga(をo)　見miたtaいiでdeすsu。– I want to watch the concert on TV.

今kon晩ban、面omo白shiroいi本hon　がga (をo)　読yoみmiたtaいi　でdeすsu。 – Tonight I want to read an interesting book.

あaのno店miseでde　美utsukuしshiいi　靴kutsu　がga(をo)　買kaいiたtaいi　 でdeすsu。– I want to buy beautiful shoes in that store.

In the following example, が highlights the wish of the subject and therefore suits が:

暑atいsu　でdeすsu。冷tsumeたtaいi　水mizu　がga　飲noみmiたtaいi　でdeすsu。– It's hot. I want to drink cold water.

Other particles of direction or purpose do not change in this case:

公kou園en　へe　行iきkiたtaいi　でdeすsu。 – I want to go to the park.

今日kyouはwa　朝chou食shokuもmo　食taべbeまmaせseんnでdeしshiたta。何nani かka　食taべbeたtaいi　でdeすsu。– I did not even have breakfast today. I want to eat something.

夏natsu休yasuみmiにni　ロroンnドdoンn　へe　行iきkiたtaいi　でdeすsu。 – I want to go to London during the summer vacations.

If we want something (I want + noun), such as these shoes, we can use the adjective 欲しい (literally "wishful thinking"). It is used in a scheme:


NOUN + がga + 欲hoしshiいi


If an adjective is at the end of a sentence, you should put a bundle です after it. Although it is not necessary in the spoken language.

Examples:

あaのno　本hon　がga　 欲hoしshiいi　でdeすsu。– I want that book.

新ataraしshiいiドdoレreスsu　がga　欲hoしshiいi　でdeすsu。 – I want a new dress.

大ooきkiいiアaパpaーaトto　がga　 欲hoしshiいi　でdeすsu。– I want a large apartment.

An object may be either animate or inanimate.

果kuda物mono　がga　食taべbeたtaいi　 でdeすsu。りriんnごgo　 がga　欲hoしshiいi　でdeすsu。– I want to eat fruit. I want to eat an apple.

誕tan生jyou日biにni　子ko猫neko　 がga　欲hoしshiいi　でdeすsu。– I want a kitten for my Birthday.

子ko供domo　がga　 欲hoしshiいi　でdeすsu。– I want to have children.


Negation and past tense

From the present simple tense form たい it turns into たかった.

友tomo達dachiにni　会a　いiたtaいi　 でdeすsu。– I want to meet with friends.

友tomo達dachiにni　会a　いiたtaかっkatたta　 でdeすsu。– I wanted to meet with friends.

Another form of desirable tilt is used to express the desire of third parties たがる:

彼kare　はwa　あaなnaたta　にni　 プpuレreセseンnトto　をo　しshiたtaがgaりri　 まmaすsu。– He wants to give you a gift.

彼kare　はwa　あaなnaたta　にni　 プpuレreセseンnトto　をo　しshiたtaがgaりri　 まmaしshiたta。– He wanted to give you a gift.

あaのno　レreスsuトtoラraンnへe行i　 きki　たtaくkuなnaかっkatたta　 でdeすsu。– I did not want to go to that restaurant.

昨日kinou、私watashiたtaちchi　 はwa何nani　もmoしshi　たtaくkuなnaかっkatたta　 でdeすsu。– We did not want to do anything yesterday.

私watashi　はwa　新ataraしshiいi　携kei帯tai電den話wa　がga　 欲hoしshiかっkatたta　でdeすsu。– I wanted a new phone.
