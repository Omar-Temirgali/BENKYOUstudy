Equivalents of verbs to have and to be (in places). あります/います verbs.

In the third lesson of the "Japanese in 8 lessons" course, we will deal with those constructions in Japanese that will allow us to say the familiar phrases in English: "I have something" or "where (is) the station"?

In other words, thanks to the scheme of today's lesson, we will be able to say that we have something and where it is.

To say that something is somewhere, these verbs are used:

あa りri ま ma すsu (to have, to be – about inanimate objects) and
いi まma すsu (to have, to be – about animate objects).


Examples:

資shi料ryou　がga　あa りri まma す su。– There are documents.

ごgo 飯han　がga　あa りri まma す su。– There is a rice.

子ko供domo　がga 　 いiまmaすsu。 – There are children.

猫neko　がga　いi まmaすsu。– There is a cat.


This construction is usually not a complete sentence and is used mainly to answer a question in its purest form. After all, even in English we do not just say "there is a phone", "there is a computer" - only in answer to a question, for example: "And what is on the table?" - "(There is) a phone". We always say where it is "there is" or "is": the table in the kitchen, the phone on the table.

It's the same in Japanese: to get a full offer, let's expand the construction a little bit by adding exactly where it is.

Let's describe a scheme that is responsible for the presence of something in someone.

When it comes to inanimate objects:


NOUN1 + にniはwa + NOUN2 + が ga + あa りri まma すsu。


When it comes to animate objects:


NOUN1 + にniはwa + NOUN2 + がga + いi ま ma すsu。


Examples:

私watashi　にniはwa 　 本hon　がga　あa りri まma す su。– I have a book.

妹imouto 　 にniはwa　犬inu　がga 　 いi まma す su。– My little sister has a dog.

田ta中nakaさsaんn 　 ( にni )はwa　試shi 験ken　がga　あa りri まma す su。– Tanaka has an examination.


If we want to say that something is somewhere, the following construction is used:


NOUN1 (place) + に ni + NOUN2 + がga + あ a りri まma すsu／いi ま ma すsu。


Particle に denotes the place where the object (NOUN2) is located. It corresponds to the "in, at, on" prepositions.

Examples:

あaそsoこ ko　にni　マmaクku ドdoナnaルruドdo 　がga　あa りri まma すsu。– McDonald's is over there.

庭niwa　に ni　花hana　 がga 　あa りri まma すsu。– The garden has flowers.

教kyou室shitsu　に ni　先sen生sei　がga 　いi まma す su。– There is a teacher in the classroom.


If we want to say that there is not an object but an abstract concept, such as "I have an idea" or "there is a lot of work now", we also use this construct.

In cases when it comes to the action, for example, "there will be a concert in the park today", the particle に is replaced by で.

Examples:

わwaたtaしshi 　 にniはwa　提tei案an 　 がga　あa りri まma す su。– I have a suggestion.

今 ima、　仕shi事goto　が ga　たtaくkuさsaんn　 あa りri まma すsu。– There is a lot of work now.

今日kyou、　公kou園en　でde　 コkoンnサsaーaトto　 がga　あa りri まma す su。– Today there is a concert in the park.


Negations

The negative sentence of this construction is very simple to make: the verb at the end of ます is replaced by ません. This is what it looks like:

あaりriまmaす su 　⇒　 あaりri まma せse んn

いiまmaすsu 　⇒　 いi まma せse んn

The が particle often changes to は (if it is not in the sentence before).

Examples:

テteー eブbuルruのno上ue　に ni　キkyャaッs　シshュu　がga　あ a りri まma すsu。– There is money on the table.

テteーeブbuルruの no上ue　にni　キkyャaッsシshュu 　 はwa　あ a りri まma せseんn。– There is no money on the table.


But there will be no substitution if the particle が was already in the sentence:

わwaたtaしshi　にniはwa 　 日ni本hon語goのno本hon　が ga　あa りri まma すsu。– I have books in Japanese.

わwaたtaしshi　にniはwa 　日ni本hon語go のno本hon　がga　あ a りri まma せseんn。– I don't have books in Japanese.


Questions

If you want to ask about the presence of something, at the end we just add a question particle か. The order of words in the sentence does not change.

時 ji間kan (time)　 がga 　 あaりriまmaすsu 　 かka。– Do you have time?

兄kyou弟dai (siblings)　 がga 　いiまmaすsu　か ka。– Do you have any siblings (brothers/sisters)?

Or in the more common phrase:

テteーeブbuルru のno上ue　にni　おo 皿sara　がga　あaりriまmaすsu　か ka。– Is there a plate on the table?

彼kare　にniはwa　カkaメmeラra　が ga　あaりriまmaす su　かka。– Does he have a camera?


If we want to ask the interlocutor if he has something, it is not necessary to specify the pronoun "you" / "You". You can start with the name of the interlocutor (if you know him), then the form of the sentence will be the same as in the speech about the third person. Or you can say nothing at all - as a rule, your interlocutors understand everything.

田ta中nakaさsaんn 、　 資shi料ryou　がga　あa りriまmaすsu　か ka。– Tanaka-san, do you have the documents?

( あaなnaたtaにniはwa ） 資shi料ryou　がga　あ aりriまmaすsu　か ka。– Do you have the documents?

田ta中nakaさsaんn 、　 妹imoutoさsaんn　がga　いiまmaす su　かka。– Tanaka-san, do you have a younger sister?

( あaなnaたtaはwa ） 妹imoutoさsaんn 　がga　いiまmaすsu 　かka。– Do you have a younger sister?


Questions with question words

If we want to ask a question to which we want to hear some new information instead of "yes" or "no", we will need question words here.

We discovered questions with question words in the last lesson.

In this lesson, we will look at how to ask questions using the verbs あります/ います.

As in the previous lesson, the order of words in question sentences with question words is the same as in a regular sentence. The word to which the question is asked is replaced by the corresponding question word. At the end of the sentence, the question particle か is placed.

Examples:

― 冷rei蔵zou庫ko　は wa　どdoこko　にni　あ aりriまmaすsu　か ka。– Where is the fridge?

― 冷rei蔵zou庫ko　はwa　キッkitチchiンn　にni 　あaりriまmaすsu 。– Fridge is in the kitchen.

― おo母kaa さsaんn　はwa　どdoこko 　 にni　いiまmaすsu 　 かka。– Where's Mom?

― 母 haha　はwa　病byou院in　にni 　いiまmaすsu。– Mom is in the hospital.

― テteーeブbuルruの no上ue　にni　りriんnごgo 　 はwa　いiくkuつtsu　あaり riまmaすsu　かka 。– How many apples are on the table?

― 5 itsu つtsu　あaりriまma すsu。– Five.

― 数suu学gaku 　のno　講kou義gi　はwa　いiつtsu　あaりriまma すsu　かka。– When will there be a lecture on mathematics?

― 7 shichi時ji　にni 　 あaりriまmaすsu。– At seven o'clock.
