Past tense in Japanese

Tense is a method that we use in English to refer to time – past, present, and future. If you are a native English speaker, you might not even be aware of how many tenses there are in English. Let’s think about the future tense for a moment. The sentence “I jog” in the present tense becomes “I will jog” or even “I’m going to jog” (And they have slightly different meanings! Did you ever notice?) Japanese, on the other hand, only has two tenses: past and non-past. It’s called non-past because Japanese uses the same tense for the present and future. That is, when we talk about the future, in form it is not different from the present. That's why we often have to guess by context. To understand that we are talking about the future of time is helped by context and various words like: あaしshiたta (tommorrow), 来rai週shyuu (in next week), etc.

In a neutral and rude style in the present and future tense, verbs end with ます.

In the past tense, this ending is changed to ました.


Verb + まmaすsu → Verb + まmaしshiたta


Example:

彼kare　はwa　8hachi時ji　 にni　来kiまmaしshiたta。– He arrived at 8 o'clock.

The particle "wo" (を): The particle "wo" (or "o") marks the direct object of a Japanese sentence. In the example, "I'm going to take her home" (watashi wa kanojo wo ie ni okuru), "her" would be the direct object. Examples:

コkoーoヒhiーi (coffee)　をo　 飲noみmiまmaすsu (to drink) – To drink cofee.

テteレreビbi (TV)　をo　見miまmaすsu (to watch) – To watch TV.

食taべbe物mono (food)　をo　 買kaいiまmaすsu (to buy) – To buy food.

Sentences:

私watashi　はwa　ミmiルruクku　 コkoーoヒhiーi　をo　 飲noみmiまmaしshiたta。– I drank milk coffee.

彼kare　はwa　一hito晩ban中juu　 テteレreビbi　をo　見mi まmaしshiたta。– He was watching TV all evening.

私watashi達tachi　はwa　 食taべbe物mono をo　買kaいiまmaしshiたta。– We bought some food.

Now we know how to form the past tense from a verb sentence. And if you have to say a sentence like "I was a student", "It was him"?

In this case we need to put a verb-link in the past tense です.


でdeすsu　⇒　でdeしshiたta


Examples:

あaのnoこkoろro　 はwa　私watashi　はwa　 学gaku生sei　でdeしshiたta。 – In those days I was a student.

今kon回kai　のno　ホhoテteルruのno部he屋ya　はwa　とtoてteもmo　便ben利ri　でdeしshiたta。 – This time the hotel room was very comfortable.

Negative sentences in the Past Tense

In the negative form, the verb will look like this:


negative present form まmaせseんn → changed to negative past　 まmaせseんnでdeしshiたta


Examples:

食taべbe　まmaせseんn (I don't eat)　⇒　食taべbe　 まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta (I didn't eat)

買kaいi　まmaせseんn (I don't buy)　⇒　買kaいi　 まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta (I didn't buy)

勉ben強kyoしshi　まmaせseんn (I don't study) 　⇒　勉ben強kyoしshi　まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta (I didn't study)


Sentences:

おo金kane　がga　あaりri　 まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta。 でdeすsuかkaらra、何naniもmo　買kaいi　 まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta。– I had no money. So I did not buy anything.

今ke朝sa　何naniもmo　食taべbe　 まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta。 – I did not eat anything this morning.

彼kano女jo　はwa　あaのno本hon　 をo　読yoみmi　 まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta。– She hasn't read that book yet.

彼kare　はwa　昨日kinou　テteレreビbi　 をo　見mi　まmaせseんn　 でdeしshiたta。一hito晩ban中juu　働hatara　 きkiまmaしshiたta。 – He was not watching TV yesterday. He was working all night.

Questions

The question is formed very simply - as well as any interrogative sentences. At first, we build a normal sentence, but in place of one of the words we put a question word, and at the end we add a question particle か。.

あaなnaたta　はwa　 今日kyou　何naniかka　 食taべbe　まmaしshiたta　 かka。 – Did you eat something today?

あaなnaたta　はwa　 どdoうuしshiてte　何naniもmo　 しshiまmaせseんn　 でdeしshiたta　かka。 – Why didn't you do anything?

一hito晩ban中juu　どdoこko　 にni　いiまmaしshiたta　 かka。私watashi　はwa　5go　 回kai電den話wa　をo　かkaけke　 まmaしshiたta。 – Where have you been all evening? I called 5 times.

あaなnaたta　のno　 両ryou親shin　はwa　どdoうuしshiてte　 来ki　まmaせseんn　でdeしshiたta　 かka。– Why didn't your parents come?
