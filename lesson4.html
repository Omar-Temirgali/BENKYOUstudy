行きます, 来ます, 帰ります verbs - to go, to come, to return

This lesson will be devoted to the theme of movement: where, on what and why (for what purpose).

There are two basic verbs of motion in Japanese: 行iきkiまmaすsu - go and 来kiまmaすsu - come. Depending on the method of movement, they can be translated in different ways: as "go", "go", etc.

There is a difference in direction between these two verbs. 行きます is a movement from the speaker or the place where he is. 来ます is the opposite direction to the speaker.

If we go to our home 家uchi (home) or go to the Homeland 国kuni (Homeland - the word also means just a country. When we talk about ourselves, it translates as "Homeland"), in Japanese, we kind of "go back" home and homeland, so with these words we will use the verb 帰kaeりriまmaすsu (go back)：

家uchi へ e 帰kaeりriまmaすsu – go home

国kuni へ e 帰kaeりriまmaすsu – return to Homeland

今日kyou　はwa　残zan業gyou　 でdeすsu。 8 hachi 時ji　にni　家uchi　 へ e　帰kaeりriまmaすsu。– Today I stay for overtime work. I will go (return) home at 8 o'clock.

来rai週shuu　はwa　 ロroシshiアa　 へe　帰kaeりriまmaすsu。– Next week I come back to Russia.

With the verb 行きます the most commonly used particle is へ. It is placed after the word that indicates the direction or purpose of movement and indicates the direction.

Examples:

来rai年nen 、 日ni本hon　へe　 行iきkiまmaすsu。– Next year I'm going to Japan.

こkoれreかkaらra　図to書sho館kan　へe　行iきkiまmaすsu。– I will go to the library now.

With the verb 来ki まmaすsu we use the particle にni - it is also placed after the word that indicates where we come to, and translated by the preposition "to" or "in".

明日ashita(asu) のno朝asa 、 あaなnaたta　の no　とtoこkoろro　にni　来 ki まmaすsu。– Tomorrow morning I will come to your house.

8 hachi時ji　にni　大dai学gaku　にni　 来ki まmaすsu。– I'll be at the university at 8:00

To clarify what kind of transport we get to, we use the particle で, which is placed after the name of the desired transport. For example:

飛hi行kou機ki 　でde　行i きkiまmaすsu。– I will fly on the plane.

地chi下ka鉄tetsu 　でde　行i きkiまmaすsu。– I'll take the subway.

Instead of the で particle, you can use the more complex design にni 乗 no っtてte. The principle is the same: the particle is placed immediately after the transport.

バbaスsu　にni 乗no っtてte　帰kae りriまmaすsu。– I'll return home by bus.

彼kare　はwa　タtaクkuシshiーi 　にni 乗no っtてte　来kiまmaすsu。– He'll come by taxi.

You can combine two constructions, and then we get the following word order:


Transport + でde ／ にni 乗 no っtてte + place + へ e ／ にni + 行i きkiまmaすsu / 来ki まmaすsu。


The difference between へ and に: へ is used when the focus is on the movement of the verb, and に when emphasizing the place of direction.

わwaたtaしshi　はwa　地chi下ka鉄tetsu　にni 乗no っtてte　会kai社sha　へe 　行i きkiまmaすsu。– I go to work (to the company) by subway.

姉ane　はwa　飛hi行kou機ki 　でde 　 国kuni　へe　帰kaeりriまmaすsu。– The older sister will return to her homeland on a plane.

わwaたtaしshi　はwa　自ji動dou車sha　で de　遠tooいi　とtoこkoろro 　 へe　行i きkiまmaせseんn 。– I do not go to distant places by car.

This rule applies to all modes of transport. But if we walk and want to emphasize it, we can put the word 歩aru いiてte before the verb 行i きkiまmaすsu (this is "on foot").

わwaたtaしshi　はwa　大dai学gaku　へe 　歩aru いiてte　行 i きkiまmaすsu。– I go to university on foot.

こkoれreかkaらra　毎maini日chi　会kai社sha　へe　歩aru いiてte　 行i きkiまmaすsu 。– Now I will walk to work every day.

And now let's learn another construction, thanks to which we can say about the purpose (why?) of our journey somewhere:


Place + へe + purpose/aim + に ni + 行i きkiまmaすsu。


In this case, the target may be some kind of action. And then it will be translated into English as: "I go there to do it". To do this, we will take the base of the verb without a ligament ます and join it to the particle に:

verb (withot まmaすsu )　+ にni + 行i きkiまmaすsu ／ 来ki まmaすsu。


私watashiたtaちchiはwa 　明日ashita(asu)　駅eki　へe 　 切kip符puをo　買ka いiにni　行i きkiまmaすsu 。– Tomorrow we will go to the station to buy tickets.

私watashiたtaちchiはwa　今kon晩ban　 映ei画ga館kan　へe　映ei画gaをo　 見miにni　行iきkiまmaすsu。– Tonight we will go to the cinema to watch a movie.

土do曜you日bi 、 プpuーu ルru　へe　泳oyo ぎgiにni　行i きkiまmaすsu。– I go to the pool to swim on saturdays.

日ni本hon　食shoku　のno　 レreスsuトtoラraンn 　 へe　おoすsuしshiをo　食ta べbeにni　行i きkiまmaすsu。– I will go to a Japanese restaurant to eat sushi.

If the purpose is expressed with a noun, the construction is as follows:


Place　 へe NOUN + にni 行 i きkiまmaすsu ／ 来ki まmaすsu ／ 帰kae りriまmaすsu。


私watashiたtaちchi　 はwa　公kou園en　へe　 散sam歩po にni　行 i きkiまmaすsu。– We go to the park for a walk.

私watashi　はwa　買ka いi 物mono に ni　 行i きkiまmaすsu。– I'm going shopping.


Questions

To ask in Japanese how to get somewhere, we need the questionword どdoうuやっyatてte / 何nani,nan でde ("how" / "on what"). It is placed at the beginning of the sentence.

どdoうuやっyatてte　渋shibu谷ya 駅eki　まmaでde　行i きkiまmaすsu　かka。– How to get to Shibuya station (subway)?

Also, after the word that means place, we will use the new particle まmaでde - analog of the preposition "before".

どdoうuやっyatてte / 何nani でde + Place + まmaでde 行i きkiまmaすsu かka。


Examples:

どdoうuやっyatてte　 ホhoテteルru　まmaでde 　 行i きkiまmaすsu かka。– How do I get to the hotel?

― 何nani で de　 フィfiンnラraンnドdo へe　行i きkiまmaすsu かka。– How do I get to Finland?

― 列res車sha　でde　 行i きkiまmaすsu。– On the train.

And to ask how to get from one place to another, we use the construction:


Place1 + かkaらra + Place2 + まmaでde + どdoうuやっyatてte 行i きkiまmaすsuかka。


Examples:

― 東tou京kyou　かkaらra　 京kyou都to　まmaでde　 どdoうuやっyatてte　 行i　きkiまmaすsuかka。– How to get to Kyoto from Tokyo?

― 新shin幹kan線sen　でde　行 i きkiまmaすsu。– On Shinkansen (Japanese high-speed train).

As you can see, putting together the structures in pieces, we have learned quite a lot from what we use and what we ask during the day.
