Lesson 8. How to build complex sentences

8

How to build complex sentences

In this lesson, we will figure out how to say complex sentences in Japanese:

I will not go to the cinema because I am sick.

In order to say the reason for something, the Japanese language uses the union から - "because", "because of". It can stand at the beginning, at the end or in the middle of a sentence. Let's look at all the cases.

Most often から is at the end of the sentence. For example:

暑atsuいi　でdeすsu　かkaらra。– Because it's hot.

病byou気ki　でdeすsu　かkaらra。– Because I'm sick.

明日ashita(asu)はwa　コkoンnサsaーaトto　へe　 行iけkeまmaせseんn。夜yoruまmaでde　働hataraきkiまmaすsu　 かkaらra。– I will not be able to go to the concert tomorrow. Because I am working till the evening.

彼kareはwa　私watashiのno　友yuu人jin　でdeすsu　かkaらra。– Because he is my friend.

来rai週shuu、休yasuみmi　まmaすsu。働hataraきkiたtaくkuなnaいi　かkaらra。– Next week I will go on vacation. Because I do not want to work.


でdeすsu　＋　かkaらra　＝　でdeすsuかkaらra


In this case it is translated as "therefore" and used as an independent word.

夏natsuはwa　雪yukiがga　あaりriまmaせseんn。でdeすsuかkaらra、夏natsuのno　間aida(あいだ)aida　スキーがski i ga　不fu可ka能nouでdeすsu。– There is no snow in summer. That is why it is impossible to ski in summer.

7shichi時jiにni　事ji務mu所shoにni　誰dareもmo 　いiまmaせseんnでdeしshiたta。でdeすsuかkaらra、私watashiもmo　帰kae　りriまmaしshiたta。– At 7 o'clock no one was already in the office. So I also went home.

In the middle of the sentence, から is used when we want to specify both the consequence and the reason in one phrase.

Example:

彼ka女nojoはha　最sai近kin　結kek婚konしshiまmaしshiたta　 かkaらra、仕shi事gotoをo　やyaめmeまmaしshiたta。 – Since she has recently married, she quit her job.


That is, here we also put から, then we put a comma and continue the phrase. We get this construction:


reason ＋ かkaらra、 result


私watashiはwa　医i者shaにni　なnaりriたtaいi　 でdeすsu　かkaらra、医iga学ku大dai学gakuにni　 入haiりriまmaすsu。 – Since I want to become a doctor, I will go to the medical institute.

The main thing here is to remember that the part of the offer, which contains から, necessarily expresses the reason. The second part is the consequence.

彼kareはwa　日ni本hon語goでde　書kaけkeまmaせseんn　 かkaらra、秘hi書shoにni　頼tanoみmiまmaしshiたta。– He can't write in Japanese, so he asked his secretary.


Question

A general question, that can be answered "yes" or "no", as we already know, is based on a normal sentence. At the end we just add the particle か.

彼kareはwa　日ni本hon語goでde　 書kaけkeまmaせseんn　かkaらra、 秘hi書shoにni　頼tanoみmiまmaしshiたta かka。– He can't write in Japanese, so he asked the secretary?

病byou気ki　でdeすsu　 かkaらra、コkoンnサsaーaトto　 へeもmo　行iきkiまmaせseんn　 かka。– Won't you even go to the concert because you are sick?

時ji間kan　がga　あaりriまmaせseんn　 かkaらra、タクシーtakushi i　でde　行iきkiまmaすsu　 かka。– Are we going to take a cab because there is no time?

忙isogaしshiいi　でdeすsu　かkaらra、 会aえeまmaせseんn　 かka。– Can't we meet because you're busy?
